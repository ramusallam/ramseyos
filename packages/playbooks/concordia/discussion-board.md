# Discussion Board Responses Playbook

**Workspace:** Concordia
**Workflow ID:** `concordia-discussion-board`

## Purpose

Draft substantive instructor responses to graduate student discussion board posts. Responses should engage meaningfully with the student's ideas, extend their thinking, and model scholarly dialogue.

## Inputs

- **Student Post** — The student's discussion board submission.
- **Course Name** — The Concordia course this discussion belongs to.
- **Week Topic** — The discussion prompt or topic for the current week.
- **Tone Guidance** (optional) — Preferred emphasis or tone for this response.

## Process

1. Read the student's post and identify their central argument or insight.
2. Acknowledge what the student did well (specific, not generic praise).
3. Extend their thinking with a follow-up question, connection, or alternative perspective.
4. If the post has gaps or misconceptions, address them constructively.
5. Keep the response concise and conversational — appropriate for a discussion board, not a lecture.

## Outputs

- A draft instructor response in markdown, ready for review and posting.

## Notes

- Responses should feel like a real conversation, not a form letter. Vary structure across students.
- Reference the student's specific language and ideas — do not respond generically.
- Typical length: 150-300 words. Substantive but not overwhelming.
- Always review before posting. Adjust tone and specificity to match the individual student.
