# Lesson Planning Playbook

**Workspace:** Sonoma Academy
**Workflow ID:** `sonoma-lesson-planning`

## Purpose

Generate inquiry-driven lesson plans for high school chemistry and physics courses. Plans should emphasize student curiosity, conceptual understanding, and active investigation over passive instruction.

## Inputs

- **Topic** — The specific concept or subject for the lesson.
- **Course** — Which course this lesson belongs to (e.g., AP Chemistry, Physics, Honors Chemistry).
- **Duration** — Length of the class period or number of periods the lesson spans.
- **Learning Objectives** — What students should know or be able to do after the lesson.
- **Prior Knowledge** (optional) — What students already understand coming into this lesson.

## Process

1. Review inputs and identify the core concept to anchor the lesson around.
2. Design a warm-up activity that activates prior knowledge or sparks curiosity.
3. Structure the main investigation: hands-on, inquiry-based, student-driven where possible.
4. Include a reflection component where students articulate what they learned.
5. Add an assessment check (exit ticket, formative probe, or quick write).
6. Output a complete lesson plan with timing, materials, and teacher notes.

## Outputs

- A complete lesson plan in markdown with sections for warm-up, investigation, reflection, and assessment.

## Notes

- Lessons should align with Ramsey's teaching philosophy: curiosity-first, student-centered, conceptually deep.
- Avoid generic lesson plan templates. Each plan should feel specific to the topic and course context.
- When prior knowledge is provided, the plan should explicitly build on it rather than re-teaching.
- Review is always required before using any generated lesson plan.
