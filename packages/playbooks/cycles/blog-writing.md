# Blog Writing Playbook

**Workspace:** Cycles of Learning
**Workflow ID:** `cycles-blog-writing`

## Purpose

Draft long-form blog posts for the Cycles of Learning platform. Posts should explore education, instructional practice, curiosity, and learning with depth, clarity, and a distinctive voice.

## Inputs

- **Topic** — The subject or thesis of the post.
- **Key Points** — Main arguments, ideas, or points to cover.
- **Audience** — Target readership (educators, administrators, general).
- **Tone** (optional) — Desired tone (reflective, persuasive, conversational, etc.).
- **References** (optional) — Research, quotes, or sources to incorporate.

## Process

1. Review the topic and key points to establish a clear thesis.
2. Outline the post structure: opening hook, body sections, conclusion.
3. Write the opening to draw readers in with a question, story, or provocative idea.
4. Develop body sections that build the argument with evidence, examples, and insight.
5. Close with a takeaway or call to reflection.
6. Output a complete blog draft with title.

## Outputs

- A complete blog post draft in markdown with title, body, and conclusion.

## Notes

- Voice matters. Posts should sound like Ramsey — thoughtful, direct, curious, and grounded in classroom experience.
- Avoid generic educational platitudes. Be specific and opinionated.
- If references are provided, weave them in naturally rather than dropping them as citations.
- Typical length: 800-1500 words. Long enough to develop an idea, short enough to hold attention.
- Every draft is reviewed and edited before publishing.
